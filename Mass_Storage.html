Mass Storage

Mass storage is long-term storage like disks, CDs, tapes, and flash drives. It holds lots of data permanently but is slower than main memory because it often uses moving parts. Files are stored in sectors (physical records) and organized into logical records, and a buffer helps temporarily hold data during transfers.

Magnetic Disks (HDD)

Spinning platters, read/write heads, stores lots of data.

Optical (CD/DVD/Blu-ray)

Uses lasers to read a spiral track. Good for music & movies.

Flash (USB / SD)

No moving parts, fast, used in phones and cameras.
